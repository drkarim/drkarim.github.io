In this Wiki

I maintain this Wiki to share my research work and new updates.


I study cardiac big data using computational analysis for knowledge discovery. I also like summarising the information contained within MRI/CT to reduce its complexity so it can be understood easily. A software library which I maintain and is open source can help to achieve this.

Real world problems solved in the virtual world - 3D reconstructions of the left side of the heart showing deep tissue information as a heat map


I also help researchers, who require deep scientific information and visualisation of their cardiac data, with new methods of analysis and bespoke software. Recent works include cardiac tissue analysis with Markov Random Fields and validation frameworks for cardiac tissue classification algorithms. I have co-chaired three international segmentation data challenges on medical image data sets. These challenges were designed to help the research community compare accuracy of their algorithms in left atrial scar classification and left ventricular tissue classification.


I am also interested in visualisations of 3D anatomical data. A visualisation technique for creating 2D flat maps of the heart's chamber, one which I had helped develop in 2013, compresses the 3D information into a fixed-border 2D parameterisation. By reducing the 3D geometric dimensions into 2D harmonises different shape variations of the heart in a study population, making it easy to compare different hearts.


My early research work on image analysis for cardiac MRI can be found in my PhD thesis. Going further back, research work in computer vision to model dynamic scene backgrounds in CCTV camera feeds is also available.

About Me

That is me through a Laplacian Edge detector Filter

I am Research Fellow at King's College London. I studied for a BSc degree in Toronto and then completed a PhD degree at Imperial College London in Image segmentation. I am also Honorary lecturer at Imperial College.


You can find about my research publications on Google Scholar, PubMed, ResearchGate and ORCID.
