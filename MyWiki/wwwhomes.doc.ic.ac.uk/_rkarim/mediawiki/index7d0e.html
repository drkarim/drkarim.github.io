Atrial wall

The atrial wall is composed of endocardium, myocardium and epicardium. The myocardium is a special muscle that is capable of conducting the electrical activity needed to keep the heart pumping. The thickness of the myocardium is of clinical importance, especially when treating atrial fibrillation with catheter ablation procedures.

Within our group, I am leading the effort to segment and quantify the left atrial wall from CTA and high-resolution novel MRI acquisitions. Together with Prof. Rhode, I am organising a segmentation challenge for left atrial wall from CT and MRI at thus year's MICCAI conference. Here is a website link for the challenge

Early days of a thickness atlas. This one is for the left atrium

Techniques

Simple thresholding and iterative dilation

My technique that I am developing currently for a quick and simple segmentation of the atrial wall in CTA images. The technique requires post-processing Morophological closing operation (n=2 iterations) followed by some manual correction.

Regional analysis

My regional analysis techniques for the LA wall challenge

Atlas creation

I am also involved in creating a three-dimensional myocardial wall thickness atlas for healthy patients. It involves a large number of cases. I write about the processes involved in this section of the wiki.


Miscellaneous

Machine learning

The relevant questions are:

  • Is it possible to train the machine to classify a pixel in the CT image as atrial wall.
  • In a supervised learning setting, what features of a pixel would be suitable to train the machine on?
  • Can training data be generated reliably?
    • Can pixels in 1-dilation from blood-pool be used as training data?
    • What about intensity ranges from the LV. Can this information be used in training?
