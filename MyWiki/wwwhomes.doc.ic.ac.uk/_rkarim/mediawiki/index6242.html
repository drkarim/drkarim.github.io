Reviewer 1

  • Introduction, it is suggested that the authors can briefly introduce the method in the last paragraph of the introduction
    • Only a few lines about what the paper is about is explained in the last paragraph. We can introduce the method early on, a very brief introduction.
  • Method, 2 subjects should be female, right?
    • I said 7 patents and then 5 male. include 2 females.
  • The mapping accuracy. In the results for the three regular shapes and the glass-heart phantom, it is worth to explore more about the mapping accuracy (measure accuracy, or distance measures) on the landmark points. Please show the 2D plot of
    • the glass-heart phantom, mark the corresponding landmarks on figure 6, and etc. Also, for those phantom or graphs, it is better to show the accuracy map (e.t. colormap the accuracy on the flattened 2D images).
    • Two new experiments to validate the method:
      • Regular shapes: For each vertex on the mesh, get distance distortion from its n-nearest neighbours.
      • Phantom: Do same as above
      • Distortion plot - for every vertex on unfold, the ratio distance 3D to 2D is assigned to each vertex and a 2D plot is obtained
  • How the method is initialised, I think the initialisation and initial orientation of the 3D surface (3D MRI) plays important role in the final 2D plots. This part may be detailed also.
    • The initialisation mainly occurs in the opening of the left atrial surface, the cutting surface and defining boundaries. A section may be added to explain these. Add a section on this
  • If there is a catheter, then, how this catheter will be looked like in the 2D image?
    • Only when it touches the surface - this can be explained in text. I was also thinking but not important: The tipâ€™s color intensity can depend on the distance to LA surface.


Reviewer 2

  • Arrange sections of the manuscript - Section 2.1 to experiments.
  • Add motivation:
    • We want to unfold to represent functional information:
      • Fibrosis
      • Electrophysiology
    • We want to use it for navigation:
    • Planning
    • Real-time catheter guidance
  • Extend clinical validation:
    • Same number of electrical data - unfold and show maps of electrical information
    • In response to suggestion by author to spin the paper to concentrate more on the technique, say we have now extended the clinical application of the technique to give it a balance. We have applied it to LE imaging.
  • Change title: Left atrium surface flattening and proof-of-concept clinical applications.
