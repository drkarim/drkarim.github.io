Notes

Sept 17, 2015

I have had a good second look at EPU13267. I have unfolded it now in a different manner, but I don't think you will find a major change. Sending you outputs from this.

But, before you look at the outputs, here are my thoughts:

  • Before the unfold, there is a step where I fuse your shells with our atlas. See flow diagram: unfold_steps.png
  • The point correspondence between unfold and the atlas is good, as the pictures attached will demonstrate. Both square and circular unfold. See attachments: EPU13267_unfold_point_correspond_evaluation_circular_border and _square_border.
  • But, note that in the first stage of the process, I fuse your EPU shell with our atlas shell. This registration step perhaps is the culprit causing large errors, which you had illustrated in the example from your email from July.
  • And, when I dig deeper into atlas registration step, what I find is that:
    • Your EPU shell has vertices 1 to 6735
    • After fusion with our atlas, only vertices 26 to 6539 are mapped. Understandably, as atlas and EPU are not a perfect match. (See attachment: landmark_registration_issue_paraview)
