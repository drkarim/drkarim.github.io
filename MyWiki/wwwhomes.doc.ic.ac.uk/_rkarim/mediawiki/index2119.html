Introduction

Accuracy maps generated to illustrate distortion ratios on unfolded maps. Ratio of distance in 2D to 3D is computed for generating these maps.

Experiments

  • Clinical data
    • CL: factor=30, smooth=10. Folder: phani-pc C:\PhD\experiments\kcl_data\surface_unfold\cgal_experiments\clXXXXX_2
    • COG: factor=30, smooth=10. Folder: C:\PhD\experiments\kcl_data\surface_unfold\cgal_experiments\CG
    • MM: factor=30, smooth=10. Folder: phani-pc C:\PhD\experiments\kcl_data\surface_unfold\cgal_experiments\MM
  • Glass heart phantom
    • factor = 25, smoothing = 10
    • Landmark points were plotted by transfering accuracy_map_scalars_smooth.vtk to vtkoutput.vtk of glassheart. This was possible using magic3 -- transferscalar switch. You will find vtkOutputWithAccuracyMap.vtk in C:\PhD\experiments\kcl_data\surface_unfold\cgal_experiments\Glassheart_phantom\Segmentation\surfaces: 
magic3 -11 accuracy_map_smooth.vtk -i2 vtkOutput.vtk -o vtkOutputWithAccuracyMap.vtk --transferscalar x -t1 1

The -t switch denotes direct copy (see here). And then to plot landmarks (change Ln 63, setHueRnage(0.66,0.0) and Ln. 58, 59 to SetScalarRange(0, 11)): 

seeunfold2 vtkOutputWithAccuracyMap.vtk shell_3D_fromITKSnap_scalars.vtk uv_xyz_mapping.uv_xyz_pointID_max.txt

Note: In seeunfold3 you cannot specify vtkOutputWithAccuracyMap.vtk as it defaults to vtkOutput.vtk

Note:The conversion using magic3 was necessary as smoothscalars generates a vtk that does not work with seeunfold. This is possibly due to alteration of underlying mesh geometry. The reasons were not clear at the time of this writing.
