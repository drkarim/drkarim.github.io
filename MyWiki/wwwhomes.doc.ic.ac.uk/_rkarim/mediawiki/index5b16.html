  • Drive to St. Etienne du Tinee from Nice and there are some breath-taking views of mountains, only an hour drive from Nice. Also the Drive to Briancon is very nice but a little farther north about 4 hours drive. See discussion here by someone who took the trip: 
''I cruised from Nice to St. Etienne du Tinee. I took 6202 from Nice then merged onto another route that I forget at the moment. I would need to double check my maps. I basically followed and crossed the Tinee River several times. The first day was overcast and raining. It was pouring at several points in fact. I was wearing rain gear over my leather and thermals so I was comfortable for the most part. After about 4 hours I was a bit saturated though. The last hour going through Nice was my least favorite part of the ride ;) The open roads once you get out of Nice are incredible. I'm not a fan of the heavy traffic portion of the cruise. People with scooters and motorcycles weave in and out of traffic, around cars, I mean it's just crazy driving. The scooters are more dangerous than the cars. You need to look over your shoulder before you pass because a scooter may very well be going against traffic in the other lane to pass you and everyone else. 

''I cruised through a small road (again need to check my maps) taking me through Tournefort. This was straight out of a James Bond movie. Single lane roads with dramatic drop-offs from the edge of the road with no guard rails. You definitely had to look out for fallen rocks as some of the roads were right beside a cliff. I wish I could post some pictures....''

''The following day after diving I returned to Tournefort. The weather was perfect and clear so I had to return to see what I missed from the cloud and fog cover. Words cannot describe it truly. It is just a motorcycle paradise. The roads were so curvy approaching Tournefort I rarely got out of first gear on my Yamaha TDM 900. On 6202 you can definitely cruise faster.''

''The scenery was more impressive after each turn. I would seriously drive a mile and stop for another picture most of the way there.''
