Experiments

  • Force distribution two types:
    • Primary
    • Non-primary

Primary: Depending on the first lesion delivery, a primary force map can be obtained where subsequent lesions deliveries at the same site not relevant or ignored. In the non-primary theory, subsequent lesion deliveries are considered and thus added up.

Non-primary: Oedematitis, caused due to cellular damage and inflammatory response to catheter tip heat, prevents further damage unless a large force is employed. Thus once oedema is formed, it is very difficult to obtain necrotic damage as the cell has staged an inflammatory response and it is difficult to surpass this barrier.

  • Create a model of force distribution, that contains following parameters:
    • Lesion extent as a constant diameter
    • Force distribution: Libear decay, Gaussian decay, etc.
    • Interaction at lesion intersections - alpha. What is an appropriate alpha? i.e. weighting of lesion

Research Questions

  • Assuming force maps are surrogate for 3m LE, what are the optimal model parameters for force distribution?
  • What is the relationship between T2 and Force distribution (Primary or non-primary)? Is it an inverse relationship?
