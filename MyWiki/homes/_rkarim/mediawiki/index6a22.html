Regional analysis

The wall is sub-divided into two sections:

  1. Posterior
  2. Anterior

Analysis of a left atrial wall thickness algorithm is performed on each section of the wall separately.

Using MIP data

If the MIP is available, the analysis is straightforward - extract posterior and anterior regions from the VTK surface.

Using binary masks

CT data

This is more complicated. Assuming only the binary masks of atrial wall segmentations are available, and the MIP surface cannot be constructed due to image (world) origin issues

1. Denoting LA chamber image as LA and wall image as W. The union of LA and W is computed. To do this, we use the concat program. Usage: 

concat la_mask.nii wall.nii concat.nii

Two things to check:

    • The order is important, as in the concat.nii, pixels with label 2 must be wall W.
    • The outermost pixels are labelled as 2, if the outermost pixels are labelled as 1, this means the LA chamber is beyond the wall. This can only be possible if the segmentation of the wall overlaps with the chamber. This is theoretically not possible, however, could be an error in the segmentation. To resolve, erode the LA chamber by 1 or 2 iterations.

2. The Euclidean distance transform is computed for pixels from the LA wall. We use the EDT program to do this: 

edt CTx_la.nii CTx_edt.nii

3. We identify ROI that includes the posterior and anterior wall of the LA. To do this, we imagine an equator or medial line (both axially and sagittally) through the LA.

4. We extract these identified regions, posterior and anterior from the images, using the region in IRTK program: 

region CTx_concat.nii CTx_concat_posterior -Rx1 X1 -Rx2 X2 -Ry1 Y1 -Ry2 Y2 -Rz1 Z1 -Rz2 Z2

5. And, finally the computation of the LA wall can take place. We use the comp_wall program to do this: 

comp_wall CTx_concat_posterior.nii CTx_edt_posterior.nii CTx_posterior.txt

MRI data

1. The same steps as above, except that there are inconsistencies in the labels used for the expert delineations. 1 for blood-pool and 2 for wall in most images, except a few.

2. The concatenation step is not needed, as the expert delineations consist of both atrial chamber and wall.

3. The EDT step is computed using the MRI_EDT program: 

mri_edt MRIx_concat.nii MRIx_EDT.nii

4. The regions are extracted in the same manner as CT

5. The computation of the LA wall is also the same as CT
