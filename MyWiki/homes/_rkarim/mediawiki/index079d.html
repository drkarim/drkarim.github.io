Methods

Through the work with Tobias, I found a mean mesh configuration, with optimal PV and appendage truncation, that gives a well-proportioned unfold. It is well-proportioned since the PV circles are well spaced evenly on the unfold and in-proportion to one another.

Using the truncated mesh to unfold mapping, any new mesh that was to be unfold, only a mapping between the unseen mesh and the truncated mesh had to be resolved. This mean mesh was derived from an un-truncated mean mesh.

However, this mean mesh has always been in .OFF format. This is due to the post-processing involved using Meshlab and ReMesh software.

Truncated mean mesh
M - mean mesh 
M' - mean mesh truncated 
> - unfold 
U - the unfold 
X - unseen mesh 
~ - transformation (affine + ICP) 
<> - one-to-one correspondence

Previously: 

M <> M'
M' > U 
X ~ M and not X ~ M' since M' was incompatible  
X ~ M <> M' > U 
Thus approximately: X > U

But after we made M' compatible: 

M' > U 
X ~ M' 
X ~ M' > U 
Now, a better approximation: X > U
Truncated mean mesh with unseen mesh data
Original mesh

In this unfold process, I was able to convert the .OFF truncated mesh that had a good unfold, back to .VTK. This had many advantages:

  • The truncated mesh could finally be used for affine mapping/fusing to the unseen mesh. The unseen is the one to unfold.
  • Previously, the unseen mesh was fused to the un-truncated mesh.


Conversion from .OFF to .VTK

  • Using this vtkOffReader code, I was able to convert the .off to .vtk

Circular unfolds in Jan 2017

I was provided with eight meshes for unfolding to a circular border. The meshes were all truncated at the PVs

There was a point-picking issue with these meshes, and they could not be registered using cartofuse to the truncated mean mesh described above. However, the registration worked in the reverse direction, allowing the mean mesh to be registered to the patient-specific one.

To resolve this issue, the scalars from the patient-specific mesh was transferred to the mean mesh following registration. The mean mesh was then unfolded.

The mean mesh has about 10k vertices. On average, there were 20 times more vertices in the patient-specific mesh. Thus, copying of scalars from patient-specific to mean mesh was greatly many-to-one.
